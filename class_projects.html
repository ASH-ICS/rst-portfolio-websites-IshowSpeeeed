Week 6 Rugby is a sport that contains a lot of physical contact, and since players don't wear any protective gear, they always feel the full force of the hit, which can be especially dangerous with head concussions. One of the only protective gear used is the mouthguard, which protects them from tooth damage and mouth lacerations and makes them look cool. Recently, after observing positive results in hockey, the Rugby union “World Rugby” said that they will start to introduce smart technology mouthguards to Rugby. The mouthguards will be able to measure head impacts and acceleration of a player. The data from the mouthguards will be sent to the pitch side doctors allowing them to see the impact of the hit and decide if the player should stay on or be taken off. World Rugby is making an investment of 2 million euros toward the support of rugby unions, clubs and competition for an adoption for the new mouthguard technologie. Dr Eanna Falvey, the chief medical officer of World Rugby stated: “The latest scientific research and expert opinion is telling us one thing — reduce the forces players experience on their heads at all levels of the game. That is exactly what we’re doing.” She added: “The advances in smart mouthguard technology mean elite players will be better cared for than ever before. We are taking smart mouthguards out of the realm of medical research and putting them into the world of everyday performance management to continue to manage player welfare in the best way possible.” Week 7 What is Blockchain? Blockchain is a ledge that works almost as a full history of transactions for Cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin. Each new transaction that happens with the Cryptocurrency is recorded in a block, where the information gets encrypted. Each new block attaches to the last one, making a chain of blocks, hence the name Blockchain. The purpose of blockchain: Blockchain allows for cryptocurrencies such as Bitcoin to not be regulated by a central authority, but instead to have the transactions in the currency verified by all the users, who can see all the transactions live at blockchain.com. Each computer that is connected to the the blockchain network receives a copy of the blockchain upon connecting to it. This ensures that a single individual can’t change any info on the chain, since all the blockchain copies wont allow for changes to be made. Blockchain in other industries: Currently more than 23,000 cryptocurrencies use blockchain technology, but thats not all. Recently, food companies have started to trace the journey that food products take to get to their locations. This can help with the prevention of food transmitted diseases like eColi. How does a Blockchain work? A blockchain is like a spreadsheet but not the exactly same. Spread sheets and block sheets used by the Block Chain are accessed and structured in different ways. Week 8 Apple is currently making a big effort to respond to the fast rise of artificial intelligence in the tech industry. The company, historically somewhat passive in this area, found itself left behind as OpenAI's ChatGPT gained fame. Google and Microsoft, in the meantime, launched AI powered search engines capable of generating human-like responses, while Microsoft enhanced its Windows applications with more intelligent virtual assistants. Meanwhile, all Apple's did was create an improved spell-check system in iOS 17. Apple's CEO, Tim Cook, acknowledges that the company has been engaged in AI development for an extended period. However, Apple's executives were evidently taken by surprise when AI suddenly gained so much momentum, leading to an effort to make up for lost time since late last year. This has created a lot of anxiety in the company and a recognition of having fallen short in the area.. To cover the AI technology gap, Apple has taken a multifaceted approach. High-level executives, John Giannandrea and Craig Federighi, are at the front of the effort, allocating a budget of approximately $1 billion annually to this initiative. Giannandrea is focused on developing the underlying technology for a new AI system and remaking Siri to make it more sophisticated. Federighi's software engineering group is incorporating AI into the next version of iOS. In addition, Apple is exploring the integration of generative AI into development tools and various apps, including Apple Music and productivity apps. The company is also thinking about deployment options for generative AI, whether it should be on-device, cloud-based, or a hybrid approach. The significance of this change is that AI is promised to be in the center of computers in a not so distant future, and Apple does not want to be left behind. The power plant can now generate electricity during the day and the night, as air from daytime sunlight retains heat, said the researchers.
